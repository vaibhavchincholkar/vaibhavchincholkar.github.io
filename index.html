About

I'm a Software Development Engineer at Amazon Web Services in Seattle, where I build and operate the kind of backend systems that have to stay correct and available at massive scale. My work spans distributed streaming infrastructure (Apache Kafka / Amazon MSK), EC2 capacity reservations, custom billing, and the reliability and durability tooling that keeps it all trustworthy.

I enjoy taking ambiguous, high-impact problems — "why does patching require downtime?", "how do we bill capacity to the second across dozens of regions?" — and turning them into simple, resilient designs. Along the way I mentor engineers, write the design docs, and stay close to operations so the systems I ship are easy to run.

I hold a Master's in Computer Science from the University at Buffalo. Before AWS, I did NSF-funded research on rendering augmented-reality graphics inside Trusted Execution Environments.
